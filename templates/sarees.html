What is Pure Silk?

Pure silk is a natural fiber, produced by silkworms. Being a worm-made product, the pure silk fibers are non-vegan and prone to decomposing naturally if left untreated. These fibers are processed to wash off the protein coating and woven to prepare the pure silk fabrics. Popular silk sarees like Kanjivaram and Banarasi are made from processed silk fabrics having a better shelf-life.

Why Are Silk Sarees Expensive?

From the very primitive days, silk sarees are an emblem for the higher section of the society. Rich women and renowned figurines used to house expensive silk attires in their closets from the very beginning. Even now, you cannot run away from paying a large amount to buy a silk saree from online and physical stores. Ever wondered why silk sarees are so expensive? There are two primary reasons for the massive price range.

Natural Fibers

As you know that pure silk is a natural fiber secreted by silkworms, rearing and extracting silk from these worms is an expensive phenomenon. When in raw form, these natural silk fibers are coated with a coarse protein. Before weaving a pure silk fabric, the raw fibers are well-processed to remove the protein coating.

Handcrafted Garments

Silk sarees belong to Indian handicrafts. Established artisan families have been creating these masterpieces through generations. From handweaving to threadwork, it takes about 3-4 months to complete a single piece for the handcrafters. Such an extensive effort is bound to raise the saree prices at all stores.

Factors Affecting the Cost of Silk Sarees

Besides the above stated generalized points, there are even more factors affecting the silk saree price. Though all silk sarees are costly, some of them are even expensive and luxurious. Are you wondering what increases the cost of silk sarees? Here are the factors which affect the price of these divine silk sarees.

Material of Sarees

Material imposes a large influence on the cost of silk sarees. The raw silk varieties are costlier than blended silk fabrics. The rayon-made art silk sarees are inexpensive mostly. On the other hand, pure silk sarees are natural fibers having a higher price tag.

Weight

The authenticity and price are often derived from the weight of silk sarees. With more detailed designs, a saree is likely to boast more gold threadwork, increasing the weight of the saree. So, a heavy-weight silk garment will cost you more than a light-weight one.

Types

The types of silk sarees determine their cost considerably. Artisans create various kinds of pure silk sarees differently. Some entail a dense Zari work, while some have a lighter Karigari on them. Simple varieties like the Tussar silk sarees are way more inexpensive than their Kanjivaram and Banarasi counterparts.

Borders and Pallu Karigari

borders and pallu-work are integral in raising the cost of pure silk sarees. Dense Karigari takes more time to complete than the spare silk saree threadwork. The more intricate detailing on the borders and pallu, the more is the cost of the silk sarees.

Saree Body Design

Much like the borders, you will come across a price hike with detailed Karigari on silk saree bodies. Generally, the plain, chequered, and temple design silk sarees will cost you less than those with dense flora and fauna motifs.

Kanjivaram Silk Sarees

Kanjivaram silk sarees are the pride of South India. Their sheen and softness win over hearts and territories. Also known as Kanchipuram sarees, these are among the most gorgeous and expensive silk sarees available in the market. Generally, the simplest Kanjivaram real silk saree price averages around INR 7,500. But some exceptional sarees may even cost as high as INR 40 lakhs!

The Kanjivaram sarees having temple designs and stripes are a bit inexpensive than the Butidar counterparts. As the Zari-work gets trickier, the saree prices keep rising continuously. Kanchipuram sarees with animals and flower motifs fall on the higher price points mostly.

Banarasi Silk Sarees

Banarasi silk sarees are the renowned wedding fabrics in India. These are widely available and have a price range of INR 5,000-30,000. Depending upon the Karigari on the sarees, the price points vary considerably. A detailed border and pallu take more artisan effort and time to get completed. On the other hand, a plain Banarasi saree gets finished in a short span. So, the former are kept at higher prices than the simple ones.

Chanderi Silk Sarees

Chanderi sarees are yet another fascinating silk sarees from India. Much like any other silk sarees, the price range of Chanderi varies with the Karigari and threadwork. While plain Chanderi silk sarees cost a bit cheaper, the embroidered Chanderi sarees are luxurious and expensive. On average, you might have to allocate a budget of INR 3,000-20,000 for purchasing a genuine Chanderi silk saree for yourself.

Paithani Silk Sarees

Belonging to Maharashtra, Paithani silk sarees are an integral attire in every Marathi household. The price of Paithani silk sarees varies with the weaving technique and body designing. More precisely, the more effort given to produce a Paithani, the more is its price range. Generally, you will be spending anywhere between INR 10,000-70,000 to purchase an original Paithani silk saree.

Mysore Silk Sarees

There are millions of Mysore silk saree followers in India. The decent designs and sober appearance of these silk sarees make it a favorite attire in a lady’s wardrobe. If you are willing to purchase an authentic Mysore silk saree, you might have to assign a budget of INR 3,000-7,000. Depending on the material and Karigari of Mysore silk sarees, the prices can go as high as INR 2,00,000 even.
